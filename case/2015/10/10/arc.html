ARC MAGAZINE #19

Magazine design and concept for Royal College of Art’s legendary annual publication.

The theme of the nineteenth edition of ARC, the Royal College of Art’s student-led magazine, is Contagion. The editors, Thea Smith and Kristian Vistrup Madsen both MA students from RCA’s Critical Writing programme, wrote about the theme and the idea behind it in the editors note.

”There is something in the air, maybe something in the water: 400 dead as migrant boat capsized off the coast of Italy in April. Fear is the most elegant weapon is the title of a Jenny Holzer artwork that began our call for submissions for ARC #19. The alternative to action, she says, is terror-induced immobilisation. The discussions in the initial editorial meetings were about urgency, about exploring the potential of art and writing as political force, about insisting on ‘this’ rather than ‘that’. We wanted to make an issue of ARC that could recognise an impossible situation, but through words and images begins to see ways out.”

We chose to approach “Contagion” from Vignelli’s perspective, where the designer is a doctor that cures visual disease. (Helvetica - G Hustwit.) We wanted to define this stage of pre-design that Vignelli refers to as diseased, in an aim to maintain a sense of it rather than cure it.

The stage of pre-design is a one characterised by diversity and unrefined human expression. Each of the 16 contributions are thus analysed and illustrated individually. They are then threaded together with a custom made, unrefined hand-draw typeface.

About the contributions: Nina Power charts the proliferation of the hashtag slogan in post-internet politics, and Bifo Berardi considers the potentials of poetry and disease in conversation with Izabella Scott and Oscar Gaynor. As part of this year’s interdisciplinary research biennial Why Would I Lie? PhD researchers Susannah Haslam and Peter Le Couteur take on contagion as it relates to their ongoing discussion of the ethics and aesthetics of art and design research practices.

Available to purchase at ICA-Institute of Contemporary Art and RCA Critical Writing.
