“ILL-INFORMED”

Visual identity and exhibition design for ill-informed, a platform for contemporary illustration.

Ill-informed is a student-run platform at the Royal College of Art, expanding on contemporary illustration discourse by organising workshops and exhibitions.

The identity is largely based on custom made typefaces which are built on the imprint of a physical tool such as a flat brush, ballpoint pens and a filt pen. Tools which are traditionally used by illustrators.

During 2015 ill-informed held two exhibitions and Summer Studio helped with the design of signage and posters. A Pen Can Build Faster than a Hammer, ill-informed’s first exhibition, asked students in the School of Communication to question and reflect upon the future of Illustration and Design education, challenging the system we are in today.

The second exhibition Isle of Lost and Found takes inspiration from islands where culture, tradition and practice often tend to be passed down through the generations. At the RCA there are technicians with an abundance of skills available to the students but they are sometimes isolated in secluded islands of knowledge within the school. The exhibition shows work of students who have been working closely with technicians across the college to gain insights that might otherwise never have been discovered.

The design concepts are straight forward and playful, simply reflecting the title of the shows. In the first one the pens, made by illustrator Maria Ines Gul, are building up the title. In the second the title is placed on wavy islands and two stroke font enhance the feeling of water by giving it a sense of movement.
